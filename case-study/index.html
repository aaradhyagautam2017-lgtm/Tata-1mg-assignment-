01 / Overview

Overview

Zepto's Pet Care category already offers a wide range of products, but it lacks a memorable campaign that encourages users to discover the category and return to it. This project explores how a lightweight campaign can transform a routine shopping journey into an engaging experience that feels rewarding without adding unnecessary friction.

The Assignment

Assignment

Design an end-to-end campaign experience for Zepto where users upload a photo of their pet to unlock exclusive Pet Care rewards.

The Opportunity

Opportunity

Existing Zepto campaigns successfully drive engagement through strong visual identities and playful interactions. The Pet Care category presents an opportunity to introduce a similarly engaging campaign while building a more emotional connection with pet owners.

My Approach

Emotion First

Celebrate the relationship between people and their pets before introducing discounts.

Low Friction

Keep participation simple—one photo upload unlocks the entire experience.

Native to Zepto

Reuse Zepto's campaign language, visual style and interaction patterns so the experience feels like a natural extension of the existing app.

Outcome

The final concept, Paw Parade, transforms a transactional reward flow into a campaign centered around curiosity and emotional connection. Instead of asking users to upload a photo for coupons, the experience invites them to unlock a surprise prepared for their furry friend while remaining consistent with Zepto's existing campaign ecosystem.

02 / Opportunity

Opportunity

Zepto has successfully used seasonal and event-driven campaigns to drive engagement across categories such as Match Day, Play & Win, and Bling Mode On. These campaigns combine strong visual identities, playful interactions, and clear rewards to encourage participation beyond routine shopping.

In comparison, the Pet Care category remains largely transactional. While it offers a broad catalog of products, it lacks an experience that creates anticipation, emotional engagement, or a compelling reason for users to actively discover and revisit the category.

This presented an opportunity to introduce a campaign that strengthens the relationship between pet owners and the category while naturally encouraging product exploration.

03 / Existing Experience Analysis

Understanding Zepto's Campaign System

Before proposing a solution, I analyzed how Zepto designs and structures its existing campaign experiences. Rather than treating campaigns as promotional banners, Zepto consistently creates lightweight event ecosystems that combine visual storytelling, rewards, and playful interactions to increase engagement.

This analysis focuses on identifying the reusable patterns behind those campaigns and understanding how they differ from the existing Pet Care experience.

Design Decision Impact

Because Play & Win is available to every Zepto customer, it justifies having its own standalone navigation entry and multiple screens. In contrast, Paw Parade serves only a subset of users (pet owners), so a lightweight campaign flow (entry banner ➔ landing overlay ➔ quick upload) is far more appropriate than a standalone product experience. This scoping directly informed our UI architecture.

Key Insights

Insight 01

Occasion Curation Drives Cross-Purchasing

Evidence (Match Hangout): Bundling party snacks next to projector devices around football occasions makes impulsive basket-building natural.
Influence on Paw Parade: Structured the reward landing product category rail ("For Mike" & "For Meesha") to display customized snacks, toys, and care products in one consolidated view rather than splitting them into generic categories.

Insight 02

Routine Merchandising Streamlines Choice

Evidence (Bling Mode On): Structuring beauty catalogs into routines (Makeup, Skincare, Hair Care) makes browsing self-explanatory.
Influence on Paw Parade: The reward coupons are styled as specific daily needs (e.g., Treat Time vs. Daily Essentials) to give users a thematic routine-based rationale for cart conversions.

Insight 03

Cohort Size Dictates UI Architecture

Evidence (Play & Win): High-traffic platform-wide games justify dedicated tabs and persistent game states.
Influence on Paw Parade: Since pet owners represent a subset, we built a lightweight, non-intrusive entry banner and multi-photo upload sheet that launches inline over standard category grids to keep layout bloating to zero.

Insight 04

Thematic visual skins establish campaign modes

Evidence (Match Hangout & Bling Mode): Changing backgrounds to themed colors (metallic beauty, pitch green) instantly signals an event is active.
Influence on Paw Parade: Designed a customized warm-cream atmosphere gradient skin (`#F3ECE1` -> `#FFFFFF`) for the campaign entry feed and landing screens to visually isolate the campaign mode.

Insight 05

Curiosity-Driven Entries Drive Category Entries

Evidence (All Campaigns): Banners focus on lifestyle allure and mystery rewards rather than text-heavy instructional loops.
Influence on Paw Parade: Replaced transactional "Upload photo for coupon" banner copy with a curiosity-driven entry banner ("A pawsome surprise is waiting for your pets' pack"), tapping into emotional curiosity to drive engagement.

Synthesis

This analysis revealed that Zepto's strongest campaigns do not rely solely on discounts. Instead, they create lightweight experiences that blend curiosity, emotion, and rewards into a cohesive journey. These observations became the foundation for designing Paw Parade—a campaign that applies the same principles to the Pet Care category while introducing a more personal and emotionally engaging interaction.

05 / User Personas

Understanding Behavioral Motivations

To design a campaign that integrates seamlessly into Zepto's ecosystem, we analyzed behavioral patterns rather than demographic profiles. Our goal was to understand how experience levels and household contexts drive purchasing behavior, allowing us to align campaign mechanics with user needs.

These personas directly informed key product decisions—from trust building to entry-point visibility—ensuring that the experience is highly valuable for pet owners while remaining completely non-intrusive for non-pet shoppers.

01

Ananya

First-time Pet Parent · Age 26 · Bangalore

A first-time dog owner who lacks confidence in choosing pet products. She engages in research-heavy decision-making, frequently purchasing small packs to test pet preferences. For Ananya, trust and reassurance are far more valuable than discounts.

Design Implication

Lead with trust before rewards. Free delivery is more valuable than aggressive discounts. Breed confirmation should confidently support indie dogs rather than treating them as a fallback.

02

Rohit

Experienced Buyer · Age 34 · Mumbai

An experienced pet owner with two Labradors. He is a high-frequency repeat buyer who values speed and convenience above all else. Rohit has established shopping routines and does not enjoy marketing gimmicks or intrusive pop-ups.

Design Implication

Keep the flow extremely short. Rewards should reduce friction instead of adding new interactions. Avoid repeatedly prompting users after rewards have already been claimed.

03

Meher

Multi-Pet Household · Age 29 · Delhi NCR

Manages a busy household with two cats and one dog. She acts as a household-level shopper, making mixed-species purchases. Meher needs an experience that can organize and cater to multiple pet profiles.

Design Implication

Design for households instead of individual pets. The experience should naturally support multiple pets while keeping the initial onboarding lightweight.

04

Kabir

Anti-Persona · Age 28 · Pune

A routine grocery shopper who does not own pets. Kabir expects his grocery shopping experience to remain fast and clean. Kabir has no intention of ever purchasing pet products.

Design Implication

The campaign should never become intrusive. Visibility should naturally decay so non-pet users are not repeatedly interrupted.

06 / Visual Direction

Establishing the Visual Rationale

01 — Emotional Direction

The campaign intentionally moves away from traditional, highly transactional coupon campaigns. Instead of treating category discovery as a marketing task, the experience is designed to feel warm, playful, emotionally rewarding, and calm.

By avoiding high-pressure language like "winning" or "earning", the reward feels discovered organically by the user. The goal is to make the user feel they are doing something thoughtful for their pet rather than chasing another discount.

02 — Visual Language

The visual language is designed to feel native to Zepto's existing event systems while establishing its own calmer identity. Key visual attributes include soft gradients, illustrated character assets, a warm pastel palette, generous whitespace, and rounded geometries that reduce visual noise.

03 — Colour System

The approved color palette shifts away from high-energy commercial gradients to create an emotionally comforting atmosphere.

04 — Copy Direction

Copywriting principles focus on the emotional connection between pet parents and their pets. Instead of leading with rewards or percentages, copy uses a warm, conversational, and reassuring tone.

Avoid: Transactional Copy
"Win rewards"
"Unlock discounts"
"Get 20% off pet food now"
Use: Emotional Copy

05 — Illustration Style

Illustrations follow a hand-crafted, playful aesthetic with soft drop-shadows and minimal detail. We intentionally avoid realistic renderings or AI-looking textures, choosing instead a clean flat-illustration style that feels warm and human.

Flat Illustrated Characters
Soft rounded shapes, pastel accents, and hand-drawn qualities that evoke comfort.

06 — Experience Principles

1
Emotion before Incentive

The relationship with the pet is introduced and celebrated before discussing rewards, shifting the user's mindset from commerce to care.

2
Curiosity over Promotion

Rewards remain partially hidden until the user participates, using curiosity to drive engagement instead of loud promo callouts.

3
Minimal Friction

Every interaction must justify itself. The flow is kept short and efficient to match Zepto's quick-delivery proposition.

4
Feels Native to Zepto

Every visual element follows Zepto's campaign architecture, ensuring the campaign feels like a native extension of the app.

03 — Designed for Real Households

Multiple pets are common, but group photos aren't.

Instead of asking users to upload every pet at once, each pet is added individually. This is more reliable for AI processing and much easier for users to complete.

Why not upload all pets together?

We explored asking users to upload a single family photo or multiple photos in one step. Both approaches created unnecessary friction—users would need to identify each pet manually, and suitable group photos often don't exist.

Adding one pet at a time keeps the experience simple while still supporting households with multiple pets.

Design Principles

  • Optional, never forced

Not every user discovers campaigns from the Home feed. Surfacing the opportunity inside the cart creates a second entry point at the moment rewards matter most, improving discoverability without interrupting the purchase journey.
